If you run a local business in Port Huron or anywhere in St. Clair County, your Google Business Profile (GBP) is one of the most powerful free tools at your disposal. It's a cornerstone of [local SEO](/blog/local-seo-checklist-michigan/) and often the very first impression potential customers get — before they ever visit your website.

Here are five tips to make sure your profile is working as hard as you are.

## 1. Complete Every Section

Google rewards completeness. Fill out your business hours, service area, attributes, products or services, and business description. Profiles that are 100% complete are significantly more likely to be considered reputable by consumers.

Don't skip the small stuff — "Women-owned," "Free Wi-Fi," or "Wheelchair accessible" attributes can be the detail that wins a customer over.

## 2. Post Weekly Updates

Google Business Profile has a built-in posting feature that most businesses ignore. Use it. Share updates about promotions, new services, blog posts, or community events. Fresh content signals to Google that your business is active and engaged.

Aim for at least one post per week. It doesn't have to be long — a photo and a few sentences is enough.

## 3. Respond to Every Review

Positive or negative, [every review deserves a response](/blog/get-more-online-reviews/). Thank customers for positive feedback and address negative reviews professionally. This shows potential customers you care about their experience.

**Pro tip:** When responding, naturally include your business name and location. "Thank you for choosing Thomas Publishing House in Port Huron!" reinforces your local relevance to Google's algorithm.

## 4. Add Photos Regularly

Businesses with photos receive 42% more requests for directions and 35% more click-throughs to their websites. Upload quality photos of your work, your team, your office, and your products.

Avoid stock photos — they undermine trust. [Real photos of your actual business](/blog/building-trust-online/) perform far better.

## 5. Use the Q&A Section Proactively

Don't wait for customers to ask questions. Seed your Q&A section with common questions and answers. "What areas do you serve?" "Do you offer free consultations?" "What are your hours?"

This serves double duty: it helps customers find answers quickly and gives Google more keyword-rich content to index.
