SEO — search engine optimization — is one of those terms that gets thrown around a lot but rarely explained in plain English. If you're a local business owner in Port Huron, St. Clair County, or anywhere in Southeast Michigan, here's what you actually need to know.

## What Is SEO, Really?

SEO is the process of making your website more visible in Google search results. When someone types "dentist near me" or "best pizza in Port Huron," Google decides which businesses to show based on hundreds of factors. SEO is about influencing those factors in your favor.

There are two main types:

- **Local SEO** — Getting your business to show up in Google Maps and the local 3-pack (the map results that appear at the top of local searches)
- **Organic SEO** — Ranking in the regular search results below the map

For most local businesses, local SEO delivers the best return on investment.

## The Three Pillars of Local SEO

For a more detailed walkthrough, see our [Local SEO Checklist for Michigan Small Businesses](/blog/local-seo-checklist-michigan/).

### 1. Google Business Profile

Your [Google Business Profile](/blog/google-business-profile-tips/) (formerly Google My Business) is the single most important factor for local search visibility. Make sure you:

- Claim and verify your profile
- Fill out every section completely
- Choose accurate categories
- Add photos regularly
- Post updates weekly
- Respond to all reviews

### 2. On-Page SEO

This is what happens on your actual website. Key elements include:

- **Title tags** — Each page needs a unique, descriptive title that includes your location and service (e.g., "Emergency Plumber in Port Huron, MI")
- **Meta descriptions** — The preview text that appears under your title in search results
- **Headings** — Use H1, H2, and H3 tags to organize your content logically
- **Content** — Write helpful, detailed content about your services. Google rewards pages that thoroughly answer searchers' questions
- **NAP consistency** — Your Name, Address, and Phone number should be identical everywhere it appears online

### 3. Off-Page SEO

These are signals that come from outside your website:

- **Reviews** — More reviews (especially on Google) [boost your local rankings](/blog/get-more-online-reviews/)
- **Citations** — Get listed in business directories like Yelp, Yellow Pages, and industry-specific directories
- **Backlinks** — When other reputable websites link to yours, it [signals authority to Google](/blog/local-link-building-strategies/)

## Common SEO Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Keyword stuffing** — Cramming your city name into every sentence. Write naturally.
2. **Ignoring mobile** — More than 60% of local searches happen on phones. Your site must be mobile-friendly.
3. **Duplicate content** — Don't copy text from other websites or repeat the same content across your own pages.
4. **Neglecting your GBP** — Setting it up once and forgetting it. Active profiles rank higher.
5. **Expecting overnight results** — SEO is a long game. Expect 3-6 months to see meaningful movement.

## How to Measure Your SEO Progress

You don't need expensive tools to track the basics:

- **Google Search Console** (free) — Shows what keywords people use to find your site and how often you appear in search
- **Google Analytics** (free) — Shows how much traffic your site gets and where it comes from
- **Google Business Profile Insights** — Shows how many people viewed your profile, requested directions, or called you

## Start Simple

You don't need to become an SEO expert overnight. Start with these three steps:

1. Claim and optimize your Google Business Profile
2. Make sure each page on your website has a unique title tag with your location
3. Ask your five happiest customers for a Google review this week

That alone will put you ahead of most local competitors.
