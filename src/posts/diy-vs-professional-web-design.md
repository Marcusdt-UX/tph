As a web design agency, you'd expect us to say "always hire a professional." But we believe in honesty: sometimes a DIY website builder is the right choice. And sometimes it's a trap that costs more than it saves.

Here's a straightforward comparison to help you decide.

## When DIY Makes Sense

### You're Just Starting Out
If your business is brand new and you need something online fast with minimal budget, a basic Squarespace or Wix site is better than no site at all. Get something up, start generating revenue, and invest in a professional site when the business can support it.

### It's a Simple Informational Site
If you just need a few pages — about, services, contact — and your business doesn't depend on generating leads from your website, a template-based builder can work fine.

### You Enjoy the Process
Some business owners genuinely enjoy building and tweaking their website. If that's you and you have the time, DIY can be rewarding.

## When DIY Costs You More Than It Saves

### Your Time Has Value
A professional designer might spend 40 hours on your site. As a business owner learning Wix from scratch, you might spend 80-100 hours getting a result that's "good enough." At what hourly rate do you value your time?

If you earn $75/hour doing your actual work, those 80 hours of DIY web building have an opportunity cost of $6,000 — which could have paid for a professional site.

### You Need to Generate Leads
If your website is a primary source of business — customers searching, finding your site, and contacting you — a DIY site often underperforms. Professional designers understand:

- **[Conversion optimization](/blog/calls-to-action-that-convert/)** — Where to place calls to action, how to structure a page for decision-making
- **[SEO best practices](/blog/seo-basics-local-businesses/)** — Title tags, schema markup, page speed, mobile optimization
- **User experience** — Information hierarchy, visual flow, reducing friction

These skills take years to develop. A template can't replicate them.

### You're in a Competitive Market
If your competitors have professional websites and you're using a basic Wix template, the difference is visible to customers. First impressions are formed in milliseconds.

## The Real Costs: Side by Side

### DIY Website Builder
- **Monthly cost:** $15-40/month for the builder subscription
- **Annual cost:** $180-480 + your domain renewal
- **Your time:** 40-100+ hours initially, plus ongoing maintenance
- **Hidden costs:** Premium templates ($50-200), stock photos, third-party apps/plugins
- **Limitations:** Template constraints, slower performance, limited SEO control, platform lock-in

### Professional Web Design
- **Upfront cost:** $2,000-8,000 for a small business site
- **Ongoing cost:** $50-200/month for hosting and maintenance (optional)
- **Your time:** 5-10 hours for meetings and content review
- **What's included:** Custom design, SEO setup, mobile optimization, performance tuning, training

### The Math Over 3 Years
- **DIY:** ~$1,200 in platform fees + 100+ hours of your time
- **Professional:** ~$3,500 one-time + $150/month maintenance = ~$8,900

The professional route costs more in dollars but dramatically less in time and typically generates more revenue through better conversion rates and SEO.

## What About the Middle Ground?

A growing option: **hire a professional to set up your site on a platform you can manage.** This gives you:

- Professional design and strategy upfront
- The ability to update content yourself going forward
- Lower long-term costs than full ongoing management

We offer this exact approach — we design and build your site, then hand you the keys with training so you can manage your own content.

## Questions to Ask Yourself

1. **Is my website a critical business tool or just a digital business card?**
2. **How much is my time worth per hour?**
3. **Are my competitors' sites noticeably better than what I could build myself?**
4. **Do I need my website to generate leads or just exist?**
5. **Am I willing to learn SEO, performance optimization, and conversion best practices?**

If your website is a key revenue driver, professional design almost always delivers better ROI. If it's just a basic presence, DIY can work. Either way, understanding the [differences between WordPress and custom websites](/blog/wordpress-vs-custom-websites/) can help you make a smarter decision.
